About Me

My name is Adam Evans. The whole point of this site was a project for my web-design class. This was an attempt to show off some features of accessible design, and make a pretty presentable and professional looking website. This website is also a first for me in being much more mobile friendly than previous web projects I've worked on.

This site should be usable for screen readers. All images have ALT text, and all videos have captions added for those who cannot hear well or are in a place where sound would be inconvenient. I also spent some time testing this site with a color inverter. A black-and-white/gray color scheme is very visible to many people and has high contrast. However I also know that those colors aren't always the most visible. The site works well with a color-inverter, though some images may look funny.

I used the Chrome extension Invert! to test inverting colors on the site. I almost think it would look better if this was the default.

Lastly, the site was inspired partially by neumorphism. This design language has a lot to hate about it, but I thought adding some subtle borders would make a huge difference to make the overall design more visually appealing and easier to see.
